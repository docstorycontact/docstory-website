# Brittany Stelmar, MS2 — Touro College of Osteopathic Medicine

**Q: What does your typical day of medical school look like?**

"Lecture all morning, lunch break, and then OMM/pathology/anatomy/histology/neuro labs in the afternoon."

**Q: How do your classes and lectures compare to those at your undergraduate institution?**

Touro employs a flipped classroom model requiring students to watch lecture videos beforehand. Class time includes clicker questions with clinical scenarios. UCLA's approach differed — lectures were straightforward with clicker questions primarily for attendance.

**Q: What have you liked about the teaching practices at your institution?**

Stelmar appreciates professor involvement and proactive tutoring support. She values the flipped classroom but notes it becomes overwhelming with excessive video assignments. She suggests reducing video lengths and making attendance optional for those catching up on materials.

**Q: What are the most important things to learn about a medical school when deciding fit?**

"I think location is important. I knew I didn't want to be in the heart of a huge city to avoid distractions, but I also didn't want to be completely in the middle of nowhere."

**Q: Can you describe the area surrounding your school?**

The campus is in Middletown, NY, approximately one hour north of New York City. The location balances accessibility to the city with minimal distractions from studies.

**Q: What organizations are you involved in?**

She is a member of Sigma Sigma Phi, a national osteopathic medicine honors fraternity, and serves as treasurer of the Health & Wellness Club.

**Q: How have you been able to develop clinical skills?**

First-year instruction was classroom-based. Physical Diagnosis classes provided hands-on experience. Students also participate in community health fairs and mission trips offering practical skill application.

**Q: What is one way your outlook on medicine has changed?**

"Getting through a year of medical school was exhausting! It gave me a newfound respect for doctors and everything they had to go through."

**Q: What resources have been most useful to you?**

She recommends First Aid for board preparation and emphasizes Sketchy's value: "SKETCHY IS AMAZING for microbiology & pharmacology, and I wouldn't have survived without it."

**Q: If a young undergraduate came to you asking about the best major, what would you say?**

She recommends science majors like biology or physiology, noting that entering medical school without background knowledge would significantly increase difficulty and workload.

**Q: What is your favorite school event?**

"Winter formal!!! It's a big formal dance at a fancy venue with really good food & drinks."

**Q: What do you wish you had known as an undergraduate?**

She wished she had understood osteopathic medicine better beforehand. Working as an ER technician alongside D.O.s revealed that osteopathic physicians work alongside M.D.s identically, with OMM training as the primary distinction. She now values OMM classes as a comprehensive supplement to her education.
