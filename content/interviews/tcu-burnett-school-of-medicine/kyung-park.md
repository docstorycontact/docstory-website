# Kyung Park, MS2 — TCU Burnett School of Medicine

**Q: What does your typical day of medical school look like?**

During pre-clinicals, the schedule included: Monday — Mechanism of Health and Disease Content Application Sessions (MHD CAS) 9 AM–12 PM plus white space; Tuesday — Longitudinal Integrated Clerkship 1 (LIC 1) or Scholarly Pursuit and Thesis (SPT) 1–3 PM; Wednesday — MHD CAS 9 AM–12 PM, lunch 12–1 PM, Clinical Skills or other courses 1–5 PM; Thursday and Friday — MHD CAS 9 AM–12 PM with white space. Additional courses included Future Accelerators of Medicine and Beyond (FAB). She has now transitioned to clinical experiences with 8–5 PM classes.

**Q: How do your classes and lectures compare to those at your undergraduate institution?**

"I think undergraduate material was harder than medical school in terms of material difficulty; however, the sheer amount of material that you are expected to learn is a lot more." The pass/fail grading reduces stress, and the smaller class of 60 students means "faculty are incredibly invested in your success."

**Q: How has your approach to learning/studying changed since undergrad?**

Kyung "procrastinate[s] a lot less" and has "become more of an independent learner." Medical school encourages customized studying and personal responsibility for education.

**Q: How would you describe the student culture at your school?**

The culture is "welcoming and students are willing to help support each other." Key features include big-little mentorship pairs, assignment to one of six houses with coaches, and access to TCU's main campus for undergraduate events like football tailgates.

**Q: Can you describe Fort Worth and what you do for fun?**

Fort Worth is "a hidden gem" offering big-city resources with small-town community feel. Activities include the stockyards, West 7th nightlife, museums, Fort Worth Zoo, and botanical gardens. Dallas is 30–40 minutes away.

**Q: What would you consider strengths of your medical school?**

Independence during phase 1 with "white space" for activities like rock climbing and sand volleyball; early clinical exposure; NBME tests only — allowing early board preparation.

**Q: What resources have been most useful to you?**

Kyung used "B&B, Anki, Pathoma, Amboss, and Scholar Rx practice questions." The routine involved watching videos, creating Anki cards, and practicing with questions before exams.

**Q: How have you been able to develop clinical skills?**

Clinical Skills class teaches history-taking and physical exams using standardized patients and simulations. A final OSCE and head-to-toe exam check occur before clinical rotations.

**Q: What do you wish you had known as an undergraduate?**

"Travel more and have fun! I kept thinking that I needed to grind nonstop." Balance is essential, and "take care of yourself physically, emotionally, and mentally."

**Q: How has self-doubt affected you?**

Kyung overcame self-doubt by "being kinder to myself and reframing my mindset," accepting "not knowing everything" while striving to improve.

**Q: How do you maintain your mental health?**

TCU provides "a counselor who is designated for medical students only," Headspace meditation app, and coach support. "The most important thing for me has been having a solid support system."
