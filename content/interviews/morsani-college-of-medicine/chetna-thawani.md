# Chetna Thawani, M2 — USF Morsani College of Medicine

**Q: What does your typical day of medical school look like?**

"MWF we have lectures 8-2. Tuesday mornings we have SELECT, which is a course focused on things like learning leadership in the healthcare system and emotional intelligence. Tuesday afternoon we have DCE, which is essentially like clerkships but a little less intense / just for practice and just for a day a week. Thursday we have a class called Doctoring where we conduct patient histories and physicals on standardized patients and practice more clinical skills."

**Q: How do your classes and lectures compare to those at your undergraduate institution?**

"They're similar to more advanced courses in my undergrad, where one lecture is basically an entire topic in the field. Each professor is an expert in their subject."

**Q: How has your approach to learning/studying changed since undergrad?**

"I used to write out all my notes, and just read them through a lot. I've since moved towards more active studying — Anki, practice questions, making graphs/outlines instead of writing out every word."

**Q: How would you describe the student culture at your school?**

"We have these things kind of like Hogwarts houses, called Collegias, which are 'learning communities' that we're split into for things like anatomy lab, doctoring groups, etc." The culture promotes a "'work hard, play hard' mentality" with friendly, cooperative students.

**Q: Can you describe the area and what you do for fun?**

"The school is moving downtown this year" to "the more classy area; it's near the Tampa bay area and has a lot of nice restaurants and shops nearby." Students enjoy gyms, bars, restaurants, and "many beaches 30-45 minutes away." A drawback is "the parking problems."

**Q: What would you consider strengths of your medical school?**

"We have a student board that very efficiently takes the class's feedback on things like lecturers, lecturing styles, content, exam fairness, etc, and brings it to the faculty." The institution has "many diverse clubs and interest groups, as well as a couple student-run clinics."

**Q: What resources have been most useful for self-learning?**

"Zanki, Boards and Beyond, Pathoma, Sketchy. I use outside resources to help clear things up and keep in mind the bigger picture."

**Q: How have you developed clinical skills?**

"I really like that USF has a Doctoring course. I've had so much practice by now interviewing standardized patients, and practicing physicals on each other and standardized patients."

**Q: What is one thing you would do differently?**

"I didn't take a gap year, and I wish I had. I feel very close to being burnt out already, and feel like if I had taken the year, I would have been a bit more relaxed."

**Q: How has self-doubt affected you?**

"I'm still doubtful of myself, especially being straight out of undergrad, I'm one of the youngest in my class." A friend helped her realize "no one has it fully together; we all suffer from imposter syndrome."

**Q: How do you maintain your mental health?**

"I try to force time for going to the gym or out running, since I know it helps me blow off steam." She runs with a friend, which "really kept me sane throughout the year."
