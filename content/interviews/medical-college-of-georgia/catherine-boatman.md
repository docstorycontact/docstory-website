# Catherine Boatman, M2 — Medical College of Georgia at Augusta University

**Q: What does your typical day of medical school look like?**

Catherine wakes up around 5:00 AM and makes Anki cards as the lecture is going along, staying engaged by building her card deck in real time. This strategy keeps her focused during lectures and builds her review materials simultaneously.

**Q: How has your approach to learning/studying changed since undergrad?**

Anki became transformative for her studying. "The people I know who are successful with Anki don't use anything else to study." She built a consistent spaced repetition practice that replaced traditional note-taking and re-reading.

**Q: What would you consider strengths of your medical school?**

MCG is one of the largest medical schools in the country and provides extensive clinical exposure across Augusta University Health. The institution has deep roots in Georgia and trains a significant portion of the state's physicians, giving graduates strong placement throughout the region.

**Q: Can you describe Augusta and what you do for fun?**

Augusta is known as a mid-sized Southern city with a lower cost of living compared to major metropolitan areas. The city is best known for the Masters Golf Tournament. Students appreciate the affordability and relative quiet that allows them to focus on studies.

**Q: What resources have been most useful to you?**

Anki is her primary and essential tool. She also uses board review resources to supplement her card-based learning routine.

**Q: How do you maintain your mental health?**

She emphasizes making time for activities outside of medical school and not letting the demands of the curriculum consume every waking hour.
