# Bao Vu, MS1 — California University of Science and Medicine

**Q: What does your typical day of medical school look like?**

Bao's schedule reflects the demands of a newer medical school focused on community health. Classes run throughout the morning and afternoon with significant self-study time built around video resources and question banks.

**Q: How has your approach to learning/studying changed since undergrad?**

He has adopted a highly structured routine centered on Boards and Beyond, Pathoma, and Zanki flashcard decks. These resources replaced the traditional lecture-note approach he used in undergrad.

**Q: What would you consider strengths of your medical school?**

CUSM is a newer school with a community-focused mission and a modern integrated curriculum. The small class size allows for personalized faculty attention and a close-knit student community.

**Q: Can you describe the area surrounding your school?**

The school is located in the Inland Empire of Southern California, close to San Bernardino. The location provides access to a diverse patient population and proximity to Los Angeles and outdoor recreation in the nearby mountains.

**Q: What resources have been most useful to you?**

Boards and Beyond, Pathoma, and Zanki (an Anki deck set for medical school). These three resources form the core of his board preparation strategy.

**Q: What advice would you give about the interview process?**

Prepare thoroughly, know your application well, and be authentic. Research each school's mission and values before interviewing so you can speak specifically to why you are a good fit.

**Q: How do you maintain your mental health?**

He emphasizes maintaining perspective and remembering that medical school is a marathon, not a sprint. Taking breaks and staying connected to people outside of medicine helps maintain balance.
