# Regine Goh, MS3 — Northwestern University Feinberg School of Medicine

**Q: What does your typical day of medical school look like?**

"During 3rd year, my typical day varies widely depending on the rotation and site. I may spend most of my day in the OR, do inpatient floor work, or see patients in an outpatient clinic." She notes that classes are scattered throughout rotations and students attend teaching conferences with their teams.

**Q: How do your classes and lectures compare to those at your undergraduate institution?**

Medical school classes included lectures and small group work like problem-based learning. She found them "far more interactive and personal" than her large public undergraduate experience.

**Q: What are the most important things to learn about a medical school when deciding fit?**

Location was significant for Regine. She valued "convenience, transportation, cost of living" and experiencing city living. She suggests curriculum flexibility matters less than actually experiencing it firsthand.

**Q: Description of Chicago and what you like/dislike?**

"Living in Chicago has been an incredible experience. I love the convenience of living in a big city without it being prohibitively expensive." Her main complaint is the urban environment makes her miss "landscapes and hiking in my free time."

**Q: What organizations are you involved in?**

During first two years, she volunteered at student-run free clinics throughout Chicago, working with underserved communities.

**Q: How have you developed clinical skills in first and second year?**

Students gain early clinical exposure through preceptorships and education-centered medical homes (ECMHs) twice monthly from the beginning. She also notes standardized patient sessions throughout the first two years.

**Q: Advice on choosing a major for pre-med students?**

"As long as you complete the pre-med requirements, there is no best major." She recommends selecting a major where pre-med requirements overlap with major requirements.

**Q: What is your favorite event?**

In Vivo, a student-run annual sketch comedy show. She appreciates "how talented my classmates are."

**Q: What do you wish you'd known?**

"To those currently applying and feeling stressed, it will get better, I promise!"
