# Alexander Worix, MS2 — Southern Illinois University School of Medicine

**Q: What does your typical day of medical school look like?**

SIU uses a unique problem-based learning curriculum. Tutor group sessions give students the opportunity to discuss high-yield content, and there is early clinical exposure beginning with standardized patients on day two of medical school.

**Q: How do your classes and lectures compare to those at your undergraduate institution?**

SIU's approach emphasizes "clinically relevant information" rather than memorizing every detail, distinguishing it from his undergraduate experience at a large state university. The curriculum is designed to train generalist physicians who can practice in underserved Illinois communities.

**Q: What are the most important things to learn about a medical school when deciding fit?**

The financial aspect was decisive: "the cost for out-of-state students was $100,000 per year" at alternative schools, whereas SIU's first year totaled approximately $50,000. The cost difference was significant in his decision.

**Q: What resources have been most useful to you?**

He credited Boards and Beyond and similar tools for helping him grasp the "bigger picture" and learn through comprehension rather than memorization.

**Q: If a young undergraduate came to you asking about the best major, what would you say?**

He advised that "any major is okay" if balanced properly, cautioning against simultaneously pursuing multiple demanding concentrations alongside pre-medical requirements.
