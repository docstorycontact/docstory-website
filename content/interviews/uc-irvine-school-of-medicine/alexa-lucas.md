# Alexa Lucas, MS1 — UC Irvine School of Medicine

**Q: What does your typical day of medical school look like?**

"Going to a coffee shop to watch lectures/study, going to meetings for groups I am involved in — and the occasional trip to Disney!"

**Q: How do your classes and lectures compare to those at your undergraduate institution?**

For some courses you have different professors for 1-2 lectures, which makes it a bit hard to adjust to lecturing/PowerPoint style. There is no better phrase for the amount of information we face than the "drinking from a firehose" analogy. It is fast and very in depth. That is why I like to podcast more than go to class — that way, I can pause, rewind, fast forward as needed.

I really like the way UCI's course is outlined. Normal function of all systems in 1st year and then pathology of all the systems in 2nd year. It's a good way to be exposed to every system twice before studying for boards.

**Q: How has your approach to learning/studying changed since undergrad?**

I used to be much slower at studying. I used to make beautiful hand-written study guides, fancy flash cards, etc. Pick one thing and stick with it. I've had to learn to co-study. I make my study guides (typed and quick) and share those with friends who share their flashcards. You have to learn to be efficient and flexible. Your study habits have to constantly adjust, but trust your process. No two people study the same.

**Q: What are the most important things to learn about a medical school when deciding which is right for you?**

For me it was location, opportunities, and cost. At first, I just wanted to go anywhere and become a doctor. But as I had more choices, I realized I needed to study somewhere with a friendly, laid-back environment and needed to be around an area that made me happy. There is nothing like a walk on the beach to destress.

**Q: Can you describe the area surrounding your school?**

Irvine itself is suburbia — not much going on. But the surrounding areas are incredible. Irvine is safe and has every store or type of food you need within a 5 mile radius. When I want to have fun, I can go to the beaches in Laguna, Newport, Huntington. I have an annual Disneyland pass for some after-class decompression with a group of classmates. I'm also close to LA and SD. I really love the location.

**Q: What organizations or activities are you involved in?**

I am VP of Education for the Emergency Medicine Interest Group, team leader/fundraising coordinator for a global health/research/ultrasound education trip to Tanzania, group leader for Elephant Mentors (mentorship program for underserved high school girls), student government education policy representative, and participated in the ultrasound and culinary medicine electives on campus. I think the global health aspect is super unique and incredible (but a lot of work!).

**Q: In retrospect, which classes in undergrad were most useful?**

Honestly, I feel that I forgot so much of what I learned in undergrad. I really can't think of a specific class that prepared me for medical school. I think my clinical experiences (ambulance/medical assistant) were the most helpful in setting me up for success in the clinical aspect of medical school in terms of comfort around patients.

**Q: What is a unique aspect of education at your medical school?**

I think just the curriculum has been helpful. UCI does their best to overlap courses. For example, you are learning about the physiology, anatomy, histology, etc. of the heart at the same time. The redundancy and different angles of the material have helped me learn.

**Q: What is one way your outlook on medicine has changed?**

Medical school has really exposed me to how multi-faceted and intense medicine is. I always knew it was a complex field, but every day I discover a new aspect of the moving parts of medicine, and even though at times it is scary, it keeps reminding me that this is the path I am meant to take!

**Q: If a young undergraduate came to you asking about the best major, what would you tell them?**

Personally, I felt like the best major is one you ENJOY and one you can get GOOD GRADES IN. I was in a really difficult major at UCLA that I LOVED, but I knew it was so difficult that I would not have the GPA I needed for medical school. I switched into an easier major that included all the necessary prerequisites. Unfortunately, numbers are important, so don't set yourself up for a bad GPA.

**Q: What do you wish you had known as an undergraduate?**

Enjoy. Relax. Trust in the process and trust in your abilities. "This too shall pass."

**Q: What is one thing you would do differently?**

RELAX. If I studied like I do now in undergraduate, I would have had a better GPA. I think I stressed too much and spread myself too thin. I would have taught myself to use my time more wisely and efficiently. Worrying is just forcing yourself to face something twice. Enjoy the ride, make memories, work hard and things will work out.
