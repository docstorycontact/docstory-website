# Anonymous, MS3 — Florida State University College of Medicine

**Q: What does your typical day of medical school look like?**

As a third-year student on clinical rotations, each day varies significantly by rotation. FSU has a distributed campus model with regional clinical campuses across Florida.

**Q: How do your classes and lectures compare to those at your undergraduate institution?**

The preclinical curriculum was fast-paced and required consistent daily study. Moving into clinical rotations, the learning becomes experiential — seeing real patients, working on teams, and studying for shelf exams simultaneously.

**Q: How has your approach to learning/studying changed since undergrad?**

Active learning through clinical experience and targeted studying for shelf exams replaced the passive lecture-based approach of earlier years. Resources like UWorld became central to third-year preparation.

**Q: How would you describe the student culture at your school?**

FSU's distributed campus model creates smaller, tight-knit student communities at each regional campus. The culture is generally collaborative and supportive.

**Q: What would you consider a unique aspect of your school?**

FSU College of Medicine has a stated mission to train primary care physicians for underserved communities in Florida. The distributed campus model places students in diverse communities across the state, providing exposure to a wide variety of patient populations.

**Q: What do you wish you had known as an undergraduate?**

Preparing for the transition to third-year rotations requires building strong clinical reasoning skills, not just memorizing facts. Understanding the clinical application of knowledge makes rotations much more rewarding.
