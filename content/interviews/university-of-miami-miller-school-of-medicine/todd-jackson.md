# Todd Jackson, MS3 — University of Miami Miller School of Medicine

**Q: What does your typical day of medical school look like?**

During the first two years, he participated in Problem-Based Learning sessions three times weekly with 7–8 students and a faculty member, followed by voluntary lectures. Clinical rotations, like internal medicine, begin at 7 AM with patient pre-rounding before team rounds at 9 AM.

**Q: How do your classes and lectures compare to those at your undergraduate institution?**

"The major difference is just the volume of material that I would have to digest in a particular day." His study approach emphasizes reviewing material same-day rather than waiting, involving early weekend study sessions and time management discipline.

**Q: How would you describe the student culture at your school?**

The culture is "laid back" with significant student involvement in events like the Dean's Cup competition against law students.

**Q: Can you describe Miami and what you do for fun?**

Miami's strengths include its "breadth of diversity" and focus on serving disadvantaged populations without robust healthcare access. The school is near significant areas of need and serves a wide variety of patient backgrounds.

**Q: What resources have been most useful to you?**

"First Aid, Pathoma, and YouTube" plus Anki for spaced repetition.

**Q: How has self-doubt affected you?**

"They chose you out of thousands of applicants… It wasn't a mistake." He maintains mental health through weight training and occasional complete breaks from studying.
