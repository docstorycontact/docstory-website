# Michele Cabeza, MS1 — Baylor College of Medicine

**Q: What does your typical day of medical school look like?**

Michele's schedule includes four 50-minute lectures in the morning, with afternoon classes. She reviews Anki cards alongside class-shared note decks to keep up with the material.

**Q: How do your classes and lectures compare to those at your undergraduate institution?**

The difficulty lies mainly in the pace and volume rather than conceptual depth. She has found the supportive Anki deck culture at Baylor particularly helpful for keeping up.

**Q: How has your approach to learning/studying changed since undergrad?**

She relies heavily on Anki flashcards — using both personal decks and class-shared decks — as well as YouTube channels for visual reinforcement of difficult concepts.

**Q: How would you describe the student culture at your school?**

Students are collaborative and help each other succeed. The culture is supportive and not cutthroat.

**Q: Can you describe Houston and what you do for fun?**

The school is located in the Texas Medical Center, the largest medical complex in the world. Houston offers a wide range of dining, entertainment, and cultural activities. Students enjoy the energy of the medical center while living in various Houston neighborhoods.

**Q: What would you consider strengths of your medical school?**

The quality and expertise of faculty, the location within the Texas Medical Center offering unparalleled clinical exposure, the collaborative student culture, and Baylor's strong Step 1 scores reflecting the effectiveness of the teaching.

**Q: What resources have been most useful to you?**

Anki (both personal cards and shared class decks) and YouTube channels for visual explanations of complex concepts. She found these supplements to be essential alongside class materials.

**Q: How do you maintain your mental health?**

She emphasizes the importance of taking breaks and engaging with activities outside of medical school to recharge and maintain perspective.
