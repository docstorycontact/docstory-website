# Prithwijit Roychowdhury, MS2 — University of Massachusetts Medical School

**Q: What does your typical day of medical school look like?**

"8AM — Wake up, meditate, eat breakfast. By 9AM — Walk to school. 9-11AM — Anki/Study. 12-3PM — Attend afternoon classes/labs. 3-6PM — Anki/Study. 6-7PM — Gym. 7-9PM — Anki/Study or any Extracurricular Activities. 10PM — Sleep."

**Q: How do your classes and lectures compare to those at your undergraduate institution?**

Classes feature greater structure and longer duration than undergrad. Block scheduling creates faster pacing than semester-long courses, resulting in more frequent exams. Medical school instructors demonstrate stronger investment in student learning, likely because many are clinicians who view students as future colleagues.

**Q: How has your approach to learning and/or studying changed since you were an undergrad?**

"Whereas in undergrad I never used flashcards or studied every day, in medical school I rely primarily on Anki (spaced-repetition flashcards) and study every day in order to try to stay on top of the vast amount of material that we are expected to retain."

**Q: How would you describe the unique culture at your school?**

The culture emphasizes positivity and support. Students feel comfortable seeking guidance from upperclassmen and faculty mentors. An MS1 camping trip facilitated bonding, and regular social events encourage faculty-student interaction outside classroom settings.

**Q: Can you give us a brief description of the area surrounding your school?**

Worcester offers restaurants, parks, beaches, and bars with distinct characters. Downtown events on Shrewsbury Street provide community engagement opportunities.

Likes: Diverse neighborhoods with emerging cafes, colleges attracting young people, and affordable living costs.

Dislikes: Limited public transportation and sprawling geography.

**Q: What resources have been most useful for self-learning?**

Anki (Zanki Deck), SketchyMedical, Pathoma, and First Aid.

**Q: What seemed to be important topics during your interviews?**

The student defined a personal narrative and program fit through self-reflection about values and career vision, then tailored secondaries and interview responses accordingly.

**Q: What is one thing you would do differently if you could go back to undergraduate?**

"If I could go back to my undergraduate years I would have adopted Anki use much much earlier. As far as learning and memory, there is no way that I find more effective to store things in my brain than with Anki."

**Q: How has self-doubt affected you, and what has helped you persevere?**

Three strategies help: commitment to a singular vision, meditation, and consulting with second-year students about their experiences.

**Q: How do you maintain your mental health?**

The student practices meditation and deep breathing, and recently started playing ukulele as a cathartic outlet.
