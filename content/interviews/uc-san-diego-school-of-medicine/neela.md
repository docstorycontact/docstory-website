# Neela, MS1 — UC San Diego School of Medicine

**Q: What does your typical day of medical school look like?**

Neela watches lectures at 1.5x speed from home, attends mandatory PBL and POM classes in person, studies at the MET building, and finds time for friends and beach study sessions.

**Q: How do your classes and lectures compare to those at your undergraduate institution?**

Medical school lectures resemble undergraduate format. "Usually all the information you need to know is on the slides" and she rarely purchases textbooks.

**Q: What teaching strengths stand out?**

Professors are approachable and provide clinical vignettes. Neela supplements with resources like Pathoma and SketchyMed.

**Q: What are the most important things to learn when choosing a school?**

The most crucial factor was observing student happiness during Second Look visits. Neela emphasizes that curriculum elements are "pretty much" standard across institutions.

**Q: Describe San Diego and what you do for fun.**

"As annoying as this may be, there is seriously nothing I dislike." She expresses strong enthusiasm for beach access and outdoor activities.

**Q: How have you developed clinical skills?**

UCSD offers POM sessions, GOSCEs with standardized patients, shadowing through ACA programs, and Free Clinic experience.

**Q: What is one way your understanding of medicine has changed?**

Neela came to appreciate medicine as an art requiring extensive practice, beyond memorization.

**Q: Advice on choosing an undergraduate major?**

Choose majors based on genuine interest rather than perceived prestige.
