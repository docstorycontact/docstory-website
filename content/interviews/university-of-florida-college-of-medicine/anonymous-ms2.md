# Anonymous, MS2 — University of Florida College of Medicine

**Q: What does your typical day of medical school look like?**

The schedule includes lectures, labs, anatomy dissections, and small group learning sessions. UF uses a blend of traditional lectures and team-based learning formats.

**Q: How do your classes and lectures compare to those at your undergraduate institution?**

Medical school requires significant adaptation in study discipline and consistency compared to undergraduate. The pace is substantially faster and requires daily engagement with the material.

**Q: How has your approach to learning/studying changed since undergrad?**

She has adopted a disciplined approach centered on Anki and spaced repetition, which she found transformative compared to the cramming she relied on in undergrad.

**Q: How would you describe the student culture at your school?**

UF has strong school spirit and community through organizations like the White Coat Company and the Grad Cup tradition. The atmosphere is collaborative and supportive.

**Q: Can you describe Gainesville and what you do for fun?**

Gainesville is a college town of approximately 130,000 people with a strong sense of community. Nearby trails and outdoor recreation are popular, though the food options are more limited than a larger city.

**Q: What would you consider strengths of your medical school?**

A pass/fail curriculum reduces unnecessary competition among students. The school has a new, modern building and a responsive administration that supports student wellbeing.

**Q: What resources have been most useful to you?**

Netter's Atlas of Human Anatomy, Pathoma, Sketchy, and Boards and Beyond. These resources helped reinforce and expand on lecture content.

**Q: What do you wish you had known as an undergraduate?**

She wishes she had been more social in undergrad and not solely focused on academic performance. Building friendships and having a well-rounded experience matters.

**Q: How has self-doubt affected you?**

She draws on family support and peer connection to manage imposter syndrome, recognizing that many classmates share the same feelings.

**Q: How do you maintain your mental health?**

Sports, reading, spending time in nature, and staying in contact with family are her primary outlets for maintaining mental health during the demanding medical school years.
