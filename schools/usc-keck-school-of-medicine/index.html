Program Highlights

menu_book

Curriculum

Keck's MD program is structured around three phases: Scientific and Clinical Foundations, Clinical Immersion, and Individuation and Transformation. The first phase covers biomedical sciences through active, case-based learning — with patient contact built in from day one, not held back until year three.

Lectures are fully webcasted and capped at four hours per day, giving students flexibility to study through spaced-repetition tools like Anki at their own pace. The curriculum also integrates health systems science and social determinants of health — preparing students to navigate both clinical complexity and structural inequity from the start of training.

science

Research & Innovation

The HTE Program (Health, Technology, and Engineering) is one of USC Keck's most distinctive offerings — pairing medical students with PhD engineering candidates to collaborate on healthcare innovation. Weekly sessions bring in physician entrepreneurs and engineers to discuss design thinking, patents, hospital operations, and bringing medical devices to market.

USC Keck ranks #28 in Research (U.S. News & World Report) and draws on over 1,300 full-time faculty. Students gain direct shadowing access to attending physicians across virtually every specialty — an experience both Keck students and faculty consistently highlight as one of the program's most formative advantages.

local_hospital

Clinical Training

Three distinct hospitals sit on or adjacent to the Health Sciences Campus: Keck Hospital of USC (401-bed private teaching hospital), Norris Cancer Center, and LA General Medical Center — a Level I trauma center and safety-net hospital. Each offers a fundamentally different clinical environment.

Students describe the county hospital experience as uniquely humanizing: patients at LA General are often uninsured and express deep gratitude for care and attention. Additional affiliated sites include Children's Hospital Los Angeles — one of the nation's leading pediatric centers — rounding out an unusual breadth of training environments within a single campus system.

location_city

Location & Community

USC Keck's Health Sciences Campus sits in East Los Angeles, adjacent to Boyle Heights and Lincoln Heights — historically underserved communities where the school's equity-driven mission becomes tangible in daily clinical work. LA General's patient population reflects this setting directly, giving students exposure to medicine that most programs can only describe on paper.

Beyond campus, students are 15–20 minutes from vibrant neighborhoods like Silverlake, Los Feliz, and Echo Park, and the culturally rich cities of Alhambra and Monterey Park. LA's sprawl is real — traffic is a fact of life — but the city's access to beaches, hiking, food, and entertainment makes it a compelling place to spend four formative years.
