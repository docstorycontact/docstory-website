Student Interview Allopathic (MD)

Gabriel Conley, MS2 — University of Iowa Carver College of Medicine

GC
Gabriel Conley
MS2 at University of Iowa Carver College of Medicine · August 2018

What does your typical day of medical school look like?

"As a first-year, every week has its own set of challenges and every day looks different on your calendar."

He describes experiencing 4–5 hours of daily lectures or required labs/discussions with exams every other Friday. He emphasizes the accelerated curriculum focuses on getting students into clinical rotations sooner than most programs, with Step 1 taken at the 2.5-year mark.

How do your classes compare to undergraduate?

The primary distinction is pacing. Medical school exams cover material equivalent to "probably a month or more" from undergraduate courses. "This ain't undergrad, baby" — prior study methods prove insufficient.

How has your approach to studying changed since undergrad?

He progressed from re-reading slides to flashcard use (Anki) to ultimately diagramming lectures showing conceptual relationships. He maintains flashcards for rote memorization like drug names.

How would you describe the student culture at your school?

"The culture at Iowa feels incredibly collaborative to me." Notable events include the Doc Dash 5K, lifting competitions, golf events, and an annual spring variety show. The collaborative atmosphere extends to tutoring and note-sharing.

Can you describe Iowa City and what you do for fun?

Iowa City functions as "a bubble within Iowa, culturally." Summer features weekend festivals and farmers markets. Outdoor activities include trail-running and kayaking at Terry Trueblood and Squire Point.

What would you consider strengths of your medical school?

Key advantages include: prestigious University of Iowa Hospitals and Clinics, strong research focus with M1 summer fellowships (~$6,000), robust Global Health programs, institutional prestige, and distinction tracks in teaching, research, humanities, healthcare delivery, global health, and service.

What resources have been most useful to you?

Pathoma, First Aid, Carverpedia (a student-created wiki), and recorded lectures with optional attendance.

How have you been able to develop clinical skills?

Clinical and Professional Skills (CAPS) courses run throughout medical school with 50+ simulated patient exercises. Early Clinical Experiences (ECE) occur multiple times per semester, allowing students to shadow physicians and practice physical exams with actual patients.

What is one thing you would do differently?

"I'm not sure that I'd do anything differently." He valued his economics degree and took a gap year working at Integrated DNA Technologies, confirming medicine as his calling. He appreciates the personalized curriculum approach.

How has self-doubt affected you?

He experienced imposter syndrome initially, particularly regarding biochemistry and anatomy. He overcame this through developing friendships, finding effective study methods, and the pass/fail grading system's perspective that encouraged personal improvement over grade competition.

How do you maintain your mental health?

Gabriel emphasizes exercising, socializing, and maintaining healthy eating habits as critical. He stresses: "Depression doesn't care how smart you are" and advocates for utilizing available mental health resources. Supportive communities and self-care prevent burnout.
