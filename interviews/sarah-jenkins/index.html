My Interview Experience at the University of Michigan Medical School

SJ
Sarah Jenkins
MS3 at University of Michigan · May 2026 · 12 min read

I had been dreaming about the University of Michigan Medical School for years. When I got the interview invite, I was equal parts thrilled and terrified. Here's everything I wish I had known before walking through those doors.

The Pre-Interview Dinner

The night before interview day, the school hosted an optional pre-dinner for candidates and current students. I cannot emphasize enough how much I recommend attending this if you're given the opportunity. I got real, candid answers to my questions from MS1s and MS2s who were genuinely excited to share their experiences. The vibe was welcoming, not competitive.

"U of M challenged me from day one, but the collaborative environment and focus on early clinical exposure made all the difference."

Interview Day Structure

Interview day started early. We began with a welcome breakfast and a brief orientation from the admissions team. Michigan uses a modified personal interview format, with two separate 30-minute interviews—one with a faculty member and one with a current medical student. Both were conversational and low-pressure.

My faculty interviewer had actually read my application thoroughly, which I was not expecting. They asked deeply specific follow-up questions about my research. My student interviewer, on the other hand, spent most of the time helping me understand the school's culture and asking what I genuinely wanted to know about the experience.

The Campus & Facilities

The tour was fantastic. The clinical skills center is state-of-the-art, featuring high-fidelity simulation mannequins and standardized patient exam rooms that are used from the very first semester. The library and student lounge areas were comfortable and modern. Ann Arbor itself is a wonderful college town—very walkable, great food, and a vibrant community life.

My Final Thoughts

U of M has an almost palpable sense of tradition and academic excellence, balanced with a surprising amount of warmth. If you're a collaborative learner who wants early clinical exposure without sacrificing research opportunities, Michigan belongs at the top of your list. I left Ann Arbor knowing, without a doubt, that this was a place where I could thrive.
